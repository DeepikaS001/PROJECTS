<h3>BMI CALCULATOR</h3>
Body mass index compares your weight with height by dividing the weight by height squared.<br>
The calculator allows you to figure out whether you are underweight, overweight, obese or within the safe parameters. <br>
Doctors also use it to determine how appropriate an individual’s weight is for a certain age and height.<br>
Although the BMI does not measure the fat percentage in your body, a high BMI could point at high body fatness.<br>
Ideal Body Mass Index:<br>
The ideal BMI is somewhere between 20 and 25.<br>
Body Mass Index Interpretation:<br>
• BMI < 18.5: Below normal weight<br>
• BMI > 18.5 and < 25: Normal weight<br>
• BMI > 25 and < 30: Overweight<br>
• BMI > 30 and < 35: Class I Obesity<br>
• BMI > 35 and < 40: Class II Obesity<br>
• BMI > 40: Class III Obesity<br>
BMI For Adults Vs. BMI For Children And Teens (two to 20 years)<br>
The BMI for kids and adults is calculated the same way but is interpreted differently.<br>
For children and teens, BMI is calculated based on their age and gender. It is often called BMI-for-age.<br>
BMI for children is expressed in terms of percentile, in comparison to the BMI of children of the same age and gender.<br>
